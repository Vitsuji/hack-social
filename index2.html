Fast Food

Fast food, also called junk food, is a food that is low in nutrients, but high in calories and highly processed. Fast food is high in saturated fats and added sugars. Some researchers believe that fast food is as addictive as substances such as alcohol or drugs.

Long-time effects

Long effects of eating a diet that is high in fast foods and junk food increases the risk of cardiovascular diseases, depression, digestive issues, type 2 diabetes, cancer and finally an early death. Frequency of consumption matters in the effects. Studies on the consumption of fast foods found that having fast food more than once a week has already increased the risk of obesity. Eating fast food more than twice a week was associated with a heightened risk of a metabolic syndrome, type 2 diabetes, and coronary heart disease.

Short-time effects

Some of the effects of eating fast food for a short period of time include a changed metabolism. Eating fast food for five days can cause muscles to lose their ability to turn glucose into energy. Eating fast food for just a few days can lead to digestive problems and constipation. A single fast food meal can lead the arteries to narrow, which in turn increases the blood pressure. Fast food also can cause a surge in insulin, which will lead to a quick drop in blood sugar that will cause a malaise feeling. One dish of fast food can increase inflammation in the body.

Vegetables and Fruit

Vegetables and fruits are important source of nutrition for our bodies. A diet that includes a high amount of vegetables and fruits will lead to lowering blood pressure, reduce the risk of getting a stroke or a heart disease, prevent certain types of cancer, has a positive effect on blood sugar, lower the risk of eye problems and digestive problems. Eating certain fruits and vegetables can even promote weight loss. Vegetables also prevent sudden rises of blood sugar, which prevents the feeling of hunger. Vegetables also can lower the cholesterol levels and reduced risks of chronic diseases. Vegetables and fruits include a large variety of nutrients, vitamins, trace minerals, and fibers that are needed by the body to function properly.

Exercise

Sport has positive, but if there is too much of it, it can also have negative effects. Some of the positive effects of sport include the ability to reduce and control weight, reduce the risk of heart disease, help the body to manage insulin levels and blood sugar, help improve mental health, strengthen the bones and the muscles, reduce the risk of certain types of cancers, increase the lifespan, and improve sleep. Sadly, too much sport can have negative effects, such as chronic pain of muscles, personality changes, as well as higher probability of getting injuries.
