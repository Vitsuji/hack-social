Obesity information Obesity and overweight are defined as “excess fat accumulation” that poses a danger to the person’s health. BMI (Body Mass Index) is one way in which a person can see if they are obese or overweight. A person with a BMI of 30 is considered obese, while a person with the BMI of 25 is considered overweight. Obesity is becoming more widespread and in recent years become a problem in the developing countries, as well as the developed countries. How to solve obesity Obesity is a problem that can be changed through the a change in one’s habits, as well as educating people about ways in which the disease works and how it can be fought. Some of the ways in which obesity can be fought include eating a diet that has little junk food, little of food that is high in sugars and fatty foods, but is high in fruits, vegetables, and nuts, as well as is high in nutrients and fibers. Another way in which to battle obesity is doing regular physical activities. Diet information Some of the most important foods that help prevent and fight diabetes include whole grains, vegetables, plant oils, vegetables, seeds, nuts, and beans. Some of the foods that should be avoided include sugar, fruit juice, potatoes (fried or baked), refined grains, highly processed foods, and red meat. The best way to practice eating, is not to count calories, but to learn to eat food at low amounts and stop consuming foods that are high in calories, but low in nutrients.
